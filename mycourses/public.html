Course Curriculum

The study of effective techniques for preparing and delivering a speech to an audience.

The process of researching, organizing, and outlining a speech, including selecting a topic, identifying the purpose, and understanding the audience.

The process of presenting a speech to an audience, including voice projection, eye contact, and pacing.

The use of techniques and strategies to influence or persuade an audience to adopt a particular point of view or take a specific action.

The study of how to use body language, gestures, and other nonverbal cues to enhance the effectiveness of a speech.

The techniques for controlling anxiety and nerves associated with public speaking, including deep breathing, visualization, and positive self-talk.

The use of technology and visual aids to enhance the effectiveness of a speech, including slide presentations, videos, and audio clips.

The strategies for responding to questions and feedback from the audience, including active listening, clarifying the question, and providing a thoughtful response.

The study of specialized forms of public speaking, including informative speeches, persuasive speeches, ceremonial speeches, and impromptu speeches.
