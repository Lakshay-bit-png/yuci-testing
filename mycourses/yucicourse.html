Course Curriculum

The study of the fundamental principles, concepts, and techniques of entrepreneurship, including identifying opportunities, starting and growing a business, and managing resources.

The process of creating, developing, and implementing new ideas, products, or processes that can lead to growth and success for a business.

The design or plan for how a company will create, deliver, and capture value, including revenue streams, cost structure, and customer segments.

The process of identifying, targeting, and reaching customers to promote a new product or service.

The process of developing strategies to sustain and expand a business, including scaling operations, developing new products or services, and building organizational capacity.

The process of testing and validating a business idea, including gathering feedback from potential customers, analyzing market trends, and evaluating the feasibility of the idea.

The process of identifying and developing a new business idea, including market research, identifying potential customers, and determining the feasibility of the idea.

The practice of using entrepreneurial principles and techniques to create and manage businesses that have a positive social or environmental impact.

The ability to recognize and assess opportunities for generating their own business ideas. Understanding of the business planning process, including how to develop a business plan. The ability to apply entrepreneurial approaches, concepts, and methods to their own business idea. An understanding of the operational issues involved in developing a new business venture, including marketing, financial, and legal considerations.
